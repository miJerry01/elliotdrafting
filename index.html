At EDGE, geolocation precision is a foundational component of our engineering workflow. Using industry-standard platforms such as Esri ArcGIS, ArcMap, KMZ/KML geospatial data, CSV coordinate datasets, and Python-based mapping tools, we implement engineering-grade spatial accuracy across natural gas, electric utility, and infrastructure planning projects. Our GIS-driven engineering workflow supports: • Structured GIS layers and engineering templates • Latitude/longitude coordinate extraction and verification • Image scale and survey alignment validation • Pipeline, station, and utility location mapping standards • GIS-enhanced Bills of Materials (BOM) and asset referencing • High-accuracy dig zone identification and anomaly mapping • Field-crew mapping packages and maintenance/operation planning documentation This spatially driven approach improves engineering accuracy in planning, trenching distances, inspection zones, and right-of-way work areas, while supporting regulatory compliance and consistent asset documentation for utility operators. Using measurable engineering data, EDGE produces drawings at a “Ready for Review / Permit” stage, incorporating pipeline attributes, geolocated infrastructure points, trenching limits, bell holes, casings, and defined work zones. Latitude and longitude coordinates ensure that engineering data aligns with real-world field locations, improving both construction safety and project coordination. By analyzing geospatial project data and past engineering drawings, EDGE can also convert legacy project information into Python-based analytical models. These models allow previously completed pipeline and utility projects to inform new designs, improving planning efficiency and enabling lower-cost, data-driven engineering workflows compared to traditional drafting processes.

SMART TOOLS

Large Language Models (LLM) + AI-Assisted Engineering Automation

EDGE Drafting Workflow Development

Since 2024, EDGE has been developing a series of specialized engineering templates and automation workflows designed to modernize traditional drafting and specification management. These systems combine Large Language Models (LLM), Python automation, and structured web technologies to transform engineering drawings and documentation into structured, reusable digital assets. Rather than relying solely on static CAD drawings, EDGE workflows integrate AutoCAD, Revit, GIS, and spreadsheet-based data into structured templates that allow engineering data to flow across multiple platforms. Using CAD datalinks and structured CSV data collections, Bills of Materials (BOM), assemblies, and component libraries can be copied, organized, and deployed directly into web-based engineering standards and specification templates. Manufacturing drawings and detailed assemblies can then be converted into scalable vector graphics (SVG), enabling web-based standards pages that allow technicians and engineers to navigate parts lists, assemblies, and documentation interactively. This approach supports research, development, and long-term engineering documentation while preserving the precision required for utility and industrial infrastructure. EDGE’s automation and AI-assisted engineering tools include: • Structured CAD datalink templates for automated BOM extraction and transfer • Web-based engineering standards that allow copy-paste integration from spreadsheets and CAD tables • Revit CSV data collection pipelines for component scheduling and manufacturing documentation • Conversion of detailed CAD assemblies into SVG-based technical standards for web deployment • Python and JavaScript tools that automate engineering calculations and data verification • Automated cross-module workflows linking CAD drawings, spreadsheets, BOM tables, and documentation systems • AI-assisted validation of drafting data including dimensions, layers, materials, and assembly relationships By structuring engineering information in this way, EDGE transforms drafting workflows into a connected digital engineering environment that improves documentation accuracy, reduces manual drafting repetition, and enables scalable standards development for utilities, manufacturers, and infrastructure operators.
